Yes, these are exactly what I needed — and I found the bug. It's confirmed: the confidence mask is computed but never actually applied to the depth map before smoothing.

The core bug

confidence.py computes confidence and mask (line ~65-70) and returns them. But look at validation.py, which is almost certainly what generated your heightfield (it produces the 1.162 µm MAE split-half number in your memory):

python
fv_odd = sum_modified_laplacian(stack_odd)
depth_odd, _, _ = estimate_depth_gaussian(fv_odd, z_step_um=2*Z_STEP_UM)

It calls estimate_depth_gaussian and throws away r_squared and fit_valid with _, _. confidence.py is never imported or called here at all. The confidence module exists, works correctly in isolation, but is disconnected from the actual reconstruction path. So every pixel's depth — including the ones confidence scoring would flag as garbage — flows straight through to smoothing and then to your Plotly render.

Why this produces spikes specifically (not just noise)

Two compounding issues once you trace estimate_depth_gaussian → smooth_bilateral:

1. Per-pixel independent fits with no neighbor consistency check. In depth_estimation.py, each pixel does its own isolated parabola fit in log-space. When the focus curve is nearly flat (textureless cavity floor, low SNR near the FOV border), a in the fit is small and noisy — the sanity check abs(k_sub - k_star) > half_width + 0.5 only catches extreme extrapolation, not small-but-wrong fits. Neighboring pixels with nearly identical (noisy) focus curves can land on completely different k_sub values. That's your isolated spike cluster in the cavity — exactly where confidence should be low (low prominence, low R²), but nothing masks it out.

2. The NaN-fill in smoothing.py uses a global constant, creating an artificial cliff.

python
median_depth = median_filter(
    np.nan_to_num(depth_map, nan=np.nanmedian(depth_map)),  # ← single global scalar
    size=5
)
depth_filled[nan_mask] = median_depth[nan_mask]

Border pixels that do go to NaN (only happens when fm_peak < min_focus) get filled with one global median value, not a locally-interpolated one. That creates a sharp discontinuity right at the mask boundary. Then smooth_bilateral is edge-aware by design — it uses the guide image to decide what counts as a real edge and deliberately avoids smoothing across it. So it preserves that artificial cliff as if it were real geometry. That's your spike ring around the entire border.

Fix

  1. Wire confidence into the pipeline. In whatever script calls estimate_depth_gaussian (validation.py and your main stage-4→6 driver), capture r_squared/fit_valid, call compute_confidence, and set depth_map[~mask] = np.nan before passing to smooth_depth_map.
  2. Replace the global-median NaN fill with local inpainting, e.g. cv2.inpaint on the depth map using the mask, or a distance-weighted local fill — not a single scalar for the whole array.
  3. Optionally add a local consistency check post-fit (e.g. median-filter fit_valid/R² and flag pixels whose k_sub deviates from the local neighborhood median beyond some µm threshold) since even "valid" fits can be locally inconsistent.

Want me to write the patched versions of smoothing.py (local inpainting) and the glue code that threads compute_confidence into validation.py? I'd need to see your main driver script (or config.py for the threshold values) to wire it in without guessing your CONFIDENCE_CFG defaults.
